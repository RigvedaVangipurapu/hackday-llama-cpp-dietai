DIETARY GUIDELINES AND RECOMMENDATIONS

Following evidence-based dietary guidelines helps maintain optimal health and prevent chronic diseases.

GENERAL DIETARY PRINCIPLES
- Eat a variety of foods from all food groups
- Focus on whole, minimally processed foods
- Balance calorie intake with physical activity
- Limit added sugars, sodium, and saturated fats
- Stay hydrated with water as the primary beverage

FRUITS AND VEGETABLES
- Aim for 5-9 servings per day (2-3 cups of vegetables, 1.5-2 cups of fruits)
- Include a variety of colors to get diverse nutrients
- Fresh, frozen, and canned (low sodium) all count
- Dark leafy greens, berries, and citrus fruits are particularly nutrient-dense

WHOLE GRAINS
- Make at least half of grain intake whole grains
- Examples: brown rice, quinoa, oats, whole wheat bread, barley
- Whole grains provide fiber, B vitamins, and minerals
- Aim for 3-6 servings per day depending on calorie needs

LEAN PROTEINS
- Include a variety of protein sources: fish, poultry, lean meats, eggs, legumes, nuts, seeds
- Fish: Aim for 2 servings per week, especially fatty fish (salmon, mackerel) for omega-3s
- Plant proteins: Beans, lentils, tofu, tempeh are excellent options
- Limit processed meats and red meat consumption

HEALTHY FATS
- Choose unsaturated fats: olive oil, avocados, nuts, seeds, fatty fish
- Limit saturated fats to less than 10% of daily calories
- Avoid trans fats completely
- Include omega-3 fatty acids regularly

DAIRY OR ALTERNATIVES
- Choose low-fat or fat-free options when possible
- Plant-based alternatives (almond milk, soy milk) can be good substitutes
- Aim for adequate calcium and vitamin D intake
- 2-3 servings per day for most adults

HYDRATION
- Drink 8-10 cups of water per day (more with activity or heat)
- Limit sugary beverages and excessive caffeine
- Water is the best choice for hydration
- Herbal teas and sparkling water are good alternatives

LIMITATIONS
- Added sugars: Less than 10% of daily calories (ideally less than 5%)
- Sodium: Less than 2,300mg per day (less than 1,500mg for those with hypertension)
- Saturated fats: Less than 10% of daily calories
- Alcohol: Moderate consumption if consumed (1 drink per day for women, 2 for men)

SPECIAL DIETARY PATTERNS
- Mediterranean diet: Emphasizes fruits, vegetables, whole grains, fish, olive oil, moderate wine
- DASH diet: Designed to lower blood pressure, focuses on fruits, vegetables, whole grains, lean proteins, low sodium
- Vegetarian/Vegan: Can be healthy with proper planning to ensure adequate protein, B12, iron, calcium
- Low-carb/Keto: May be beneficial for some, but should be done under guidance, ensure adequate fiber and nutrients

MEAL TIMING
- Regular meal patterns can help regulate blood sugar and appetite
- Breakfast within 1-2 hours of waking
- Space meals 3-5 hours apart
- Avoid eating 2-3 hours before bedtime for better sleep

PORTION CONTROL
- Use visual cues: palm-sized protein, fist-sized carbs, thumb-sized fats
- Read nutrition labels
- Be mindful of restaurant portions (often 2-3 times appropriate size)
- Use smaller plates to help with portion perception
